## People who preached about kindness

Lao Tzu, the ancient Chinese philosopher, believed that kindness was the foundation for true wisdom and connection. He said: "Kindness in words creates confidence. Kindness in thinking creates profoundness. Kindness in giving creates love."

The Buddha taught his followers to practice boundless love and kindness as a central part of their spiritual life. He said: "Radiate boundless love towards the entire world."

Mother Teresa focused on the immense power of small, consistent acts of love, urging people to start close to home. She said: "Not all of us can do great things. But we can do small things with great love."

The Dalai Lama views simple, heartfelt goodness as a universal, non-denominational spiritual practice available to everyone. He said: "Be kind whenever possible. It is always possible."
